Pilot Program Launch: Blockchain-Based Micro-Credentialing

March 4, 2025 Dr. Leyla Nazhand-Ali

TL;DR: We are piloting a blockchain-based micro-credentialing system to document verified programming skills in a way that extends beyond traditional grades. Completing the onboarding round earns bonus points on your midterm.

Dear all,

This semester, we are piloting a new approach to programming assessment that is part of a larger long-term vision for engineering education.

Traditional grading systems reduce your work to a number — a GPA, a percentage, a letter. While grades matter, they do not fully capture what you are capable of building or solving. In industry, what ultimately matters is demonstrable skill.

Our goal is to move toward a micro-credentialing model in which specific programming competencies are assessed in a structured, interview-style format and, once mastered, recorded as verified credentials. These credentials are designed to represent concrete, industry-relevant skills rather than simply course completion.

Blockchain Technology for Credential Issuance

To support this initiative, we are using blockchain technology as the infrastructure for credential issuance. Blockchain provides an immutable, tamper-resistant ledger — meaning once a credential is issued, it cannot be altered or fabricated. It becomes a permanent, independently verifiable record of demonstrated skill.

In many ways, these credentials function similarly to non-fungible digital assets (NFTs), in that each one is unique and tied specifically to you and the competency you demonstrated. However, unlike speculative NFTs associated with digital art or cryptocurrency markets, these credentials represent verified academic achievement and technical proficiency. Their value is not market-driven — it is skill-driven.

Building Your Portfolio

Over time, this will allow you to build a portfolio of verified competencies that extends beyond this course. Instead of simply stating that you took Embedded Systems, you will be able to demonstrate that you passed structured assessments in specific technical skills under proctored conditions.

Incentive for Participation

Because this is a pilot effort and your participation helps us refine and strengthen the system, successful completion of the onboarding round as well as the upcoming bonus test will earn you bonus points on your midterm exams. I value your engagement in building something that may shape the future direction of technical credentialing.

Platform Development

To support this initiative, my PhD student, Yahia Tawfik, has developed the blockchain-based micro-credentialing platform that we will use for selected programming assessments.

Please read the onboarding instructions carefully and complete the onboarding round before the deadline.

Best,
Leyla Nazhand-Ali, Ph.D.
